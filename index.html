Role Matrix

How the 14 original personas map across four platform roles, organised by fund movement and structural control.

Principal
The primary decision-maker and commercially accountable party for the account. Defines investment mandates, sets limits and approval thresholds, authorizes significant fund movements, and holds ultimate responsibility for account activity. May also execute trades in smaller firm configurations where the same person sets strategy and executes.
  • Asset managers (1, 8)
  • Fund managers (4, 5)
  • Family office leads, corporate treasury heads (7, 11)
  • Senior management in multi-manager firms (9)
  • Custodians and institutional brokers acting as primary account holder (12, 13)
Distinct from Platform Admin, who handles technical account configuration. The Principal owns the mandate; the Admin configures the platform.
Trader / Executor
Executes trades and manages positions
  • Multi-trader firms — traders (9)
  • Algo / bot traders (10)
  • Arbitrage traders (6)
  • IFA — execution role (3)
  • Broker / institutional desk traders (13)
Client / End Investor
Owns funds; may have limited platform access
  • Indirect customers of advisory firms (2)
  • IFA's retail clients (3)
  • Sub-account holders (5, 6, 8)
  • Family office beneficiaries (7)
  • Institutional clients of brokers (13)
Observer / Compliance
Read-only oversight; no trade or fund access
  • Fund administrators (14)
  • Compliance officers (14)
  • Advisory firm oversight (2)
  • Approvers — multi-sig (9, 11, 12)
  • Custodian ops + KYC teams (6, 12)

Overview

Platform Configurations

Four discrete configurations that each carry their own navigation, permissions, and mental model. A user may hold more than one configuration, scoped to specific accounts.

Two organizing layers

Roles define what a user can do — they are the testable unit. A tester logs in as a Trader, or as a Principal, and validates capabilities at that level.

Configurations define how a firm has set up the product — they are the commercial and product packaging layer. A DM configuration bundles Principal + Trader roles for an asset management firm context.

Testing happens at role level. Product discussions happen at configuration level.

Discretionary Manager
Executes + governs fund structures
Wealth Advisor / IFA
Oversees + advises client accounts
Programmatic Operator
API-first; scale automation
Governance + Compliance
Approvals, audit, read-only oversight
Each role = a discrete platform configuration with its own navigation, permissions, and mental model
Permissions layer
Determines which platform configurations a given user can access — handles multi-hat employees without muddying the IA. A user's role is a combination of these configurations, scoped to specific accounts or funds.
Design principle
Design each configuration as if it is the only thing that person does. Complexity lives in settings and permissions, not in the navigation. A trader should never see governance queues; a compliance officer should never see an order entry form.
One structural exception
The Wealth Advisor / IFA configuration must surface client account data within its own view — this is not hat-switching, it is core to the role. Implement via a dedicated client panel inside the advisor's navigation, not by blending the client's account view into the advisor's.

Glossary

Consolidated B2B and Institutional IA terminology. Terms marked New were introduced or formalised as part of this IA work. All other terms are drawn from existing Luno B2B documentation.

1 · Platform Configurations & Roles
Account Holder
The person responsible for the business account; Luno's primary point of contact for the company. There can only be one Account Holder per business. Their role cannot be deleted — only transferred.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Account Management
The set of features related to accessing information about how an account is being used and who has access to it. Includes Multi-User Access and Role-Based Access Control features.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Admin / Administrator
A permission group. The Account Holder holds Admin permissions by default and can grant them to other Operators. Admins have broad access to account settings, users, and governance configuration.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Authorized Representative / Official
Anyone granted authorization by the business via an official letter to perform actions on the business's behalf with Luno.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Controller / Approver
New
A sub-type of the Governance + Compliance platform configuration with authority to approve, reject, freeze accounts, and escalate issues. Cannot trade or move funds directly. Distinct from the Read-Only Observer sub-type, which has no action capability. See also: Segregation of Duties.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Discretionary Manager
New
Platform configuration for operators who both govern account structures and execute fund operations on behalf of clients. Clients typically have no platform access. The manager is supported by a trading team executing within defined mandates. Absorbs personas: Asset Manager, Fund Manager, Family Office / Wealth Manager, Bundle-Based Asset Manager, Algorithmic Trader.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Governance + Compliance
New
Platform configuration providing approvals, audit trails, and read-only oversight. Has two sub-types: Read-Only Observer and Controller / Approver. This role never executes trades or moves funds directly. Hard rule: a person who moves funds or trades on an account cannot hold this role on the same accounts.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Governance & Role-Based Access
Role tiers (e.g. Trader, Asset Manager, Admin, Approver) that define who can view, trade, or approve across sub-accounts or portfolios. Implemented via RBAC on top of OAuth / authorisation systems.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources; B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Multi-User Access
Capability for a business account to have multiple users (traders, managers, admins, approvers) with differentiated permissions. Implemented via RBAC; developed as a project independent of but related to sub-accounts.
Source: B2B weekly check-in; B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Operator / Business Operator
Updated
Historically, a user authorised to operate on a business account via a limited legacy "operator" system. In the new architecture this concept is replaced by the generalised User Login + Identity + RBAC model and the four Platform Configurations. The term "operator" may still appear in legacy API and code references.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2; Luno Institutional IA
Platform Configuration
New
One of four discrete operational contexts on the Institutional platform — Discretionary Manager, Wealth Advisor / IFA, Programmatic Operator, and Governance + Compliance — each with its own navigation, permissions, and mental model. A single user may hold more than one configuration, scoped to specific accounts or funds. Complexity lives in settings and permissions, not in the navigation.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Programmatic Operator
New
Platform configuration for API-first, high-volume, automated operations. Primary interface is the API and developer tooling, not a UI dashboard. Used by arbitrage traders, algorithmic / bot traders, and operators managing potentially thousands of sub-accounts at scale. Supports bulk distribution, bulk collection, and per-strategy P&L tracking.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Read-Only Observer
New
A sub-type of the Governance + Compliance platform configuration with view-only access across accounts. Can monitor, reconcile, and generate reports, but cannot approve actions or intervene in operations. May not require individual KYC if strictly observational. See also: Controller / Approver.
Source: Luno Institutional IA; Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
System for assigning permissions to roles on specific resources (wallets, portfolios, accounts). Enables fine-grained control beyond "all or nothing" authorisation — e.g. Trader A can trade on Nigerian portfolios but not Malaysian ones. Replaces the legacy operator model.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
User Login
Represents a person interacting with the product (email/password, API key, etc.), separate from the customer account (which owns assets) and identity (KYC/KYB data). One user can have logins in personal and professional capacities and be associated with multiple customer accounts via RBAC.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
View-Only Role Access
Updated
Access pattern providing read-only views on accounts or portfolios with no ability to trade or move funds. In the Institutional IA this is formalised as the Read-Only Observer sub-type of the Governance + Compliance platform configuration. May not require KYC if the user performs no transactions. See also: Controller / Approver for the action-capable variant.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources; Luno Institutional IA
Wealth Advisor / IFA
New
Platform configuration for client-facing advisory and portfolio management. The only platform configuration where clients may have direct access (view-only or full trading). Contains a dedicated Client Panel as a core structural component — surfacing client account data within the advisor's own navigation, not as a separate configuration. Absorbs personas: Financial Advisory Firm, Independent Financial Advisor, Broker / Institutional Desk.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
2 · Customers & Segments
Business Customer
A business entity onboarding with Luno. Refers to the business as a whole, not any individual within it or operating on its behalf.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Corporate Treasury (persona)
Business customer managing liquidity across entities or subsidiaries, often using Business-to-Business Sharing and sub-accounts for segregation. Typically requires multi-approver withdrawal policies and inter-entity transfers. Maps to Principal role in the Institutional IA.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Customer (general)
The entity that owns assets in the system and has a Luno customer account. In the new model the customer account owns wallets and portfolios, distinct from the user login and identity objects.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Customer Type – Business
Segment value: customer-type = "business". Legal-entity customer (company, fund, etc.) that holds a Luno Business Account. May have multiple users and more complex governance and sub-accounts than an individual customer.
Source: B2B weekly check-in
Customer Type – Individual
Segment value: customer-type = "individual". Retail customer; owns their own account and wallets; typically a single user with no sub-account complexity.
Source: B2B weekly check-in
Family Office / Wealth Manager (persona)
Manages funds across multiple businesses or individuals, often using Business-to-Business Sharing and sub-accounts / client accounts. May involve optional ownership transfer of accounts between entities. Maps to Discretionary Manager or Wealth Advisor / IFA platform configuration depending on client access model.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Fund Manager (persona)
A professional entity or individual managing client funds, often via sub-accounts or portfolios. Operates on behalf of the customer account. Maps to Discretionary Manager platform configuration in the Institutional IA.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Indirect (Downstream) Customer
The end customer or investor that Luno does not have a direct relationship with, but whose funds or accounts are managed by an intermediary. Used instead of "Shared Client." See also: Beneficial Owner.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Institution
Used in the "Luno for Institutions" context. Typically larger, more complex entities (hedge funds, prop traders, pensions, etc.) requiring institutional-grade custody, trading, and reporting capabilities.
Source: B2B weekly check-in
Integrated Crypto Services
Businesses embedding Luno in their own product (via retail Luno accounts or internal wallets) as part of their own application or service offering.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Intermediary / Channel Customer
A business entity that holds the Luno Business Account and manages indirect customers on their behalf. Examples: asset managers, advisors, brokers, custodians. Equivalent to the Principal role in the Institutional IA.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
3 · Accounts & Structures
Account
⚠ Use carefully
A generic term that can indicate a multitude of things including users or crypto wallets. Do not use alone in conversation — be specific. Prefer: Business Account, Customer Account, Sub-Account, Client Account, or Wallet as appropriate.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Arrel Technology Model
A South Africa-specific workaround for multi-account management via an external platform (Fireblocks-like). An integration pattern rather than native sub-accounts; currently supports Halogen and new business customers.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Business Account
The Luno account representing the entire business entity. Owns wallets and (in the new model) portfolios. KYB is applied at this level. The primary account from which sub-accounts may be created.
Source: Business Accounts Glossary
Business-to-Business Sharing
Pattern where a business customer shares access across multiple entities or subsidiaries (e.g. family office, corporate treasury), usually with governance controls over who can operate what across those entities.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Client Access Model
New
The three-tier model for client platform access used in the Wealth Advisor / IFA configuration: (1) No Access — advisor manages everything, client receives reports only; (2) View-Only — client can see their portfolio but cannot trade or move funds; (3) Full Trading Access — client can trade and move funds independently alongside the advisor.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Client Account
A type of sub-account functioning like a virtual account with segregated funds for on-/off-ramp, linked to a beneficiary. Considered table-stakes for institutional clients. Enables third-party withdrawals, scalable onboarding, and direct deposit/withdrawal automation. Can be created via API or front-end.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 1; Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Client Panel
New
The dedicated interface within the Wealth Advisor / IFA platform configuration for viewing and managing individual client accounts. Client account data surfaces here as a core feature of the advisor's workflow — not a hat-switch to a separate configuration. Must show: holdings, performance, allocation drift, transaction history, and a space to document recommendations.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Client Sub-Accounts
Individual verified accounts (with bank + KYC) linked under a business master account. Can be created via API or front-end. Enables third-party withdrawals, scalable onboarding, and direct deposit/withdrawal automation. Used heavily by fund managers, arbitrage traders, and bundle-based asset managers.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Fund Segregation
New
The strict separation of assets between different funds, clients, or strategies to maintain clean audit trails and prevent co-mingling. Each segregated account has dedicated deposit and withdrawal flows. Required for clear client reporting and regulatory compliance.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Segregated Management Accounts (SMA)
A front-end and API model allowing multiple sub-accounts under a single business entity, each with dedicated fund segregation and governance policies. Positioned as the replacement for using multiple standalone business accounts (Halogen → CAPE model).
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
Strategy Sub-Account
New
A sub-account dedicated to a single algorithmic trading strategy or bot, used by Programmatic Operators for isolated P&L tracking, independent transaction history, and per-strategy risk management. Allows comparison of strategy performance and independent fine-tuning.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Sub-Accounts
Multiple accounts nested under a customer account, enabling segregation of funds, beneficiaries, and governance (e.g. client accounts, regional accounts, strategy accounts, margin separation). A long-standing high-priority B2B feature and table-stakes for institutional clients. Sub-types include: Client Sub-Account, Strategy Sub-Account, and Segregated Management Account.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2; Pod-B2B Discovery workshop
Sub-Wallet Segregation
Pattern (often API-only) for algorithmic / bot traders where each wallet is tied to a specific strategy or bot for tracking and segregation, under a single verified business. Closely related to Strategy Sub-Account.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources
4 · Data Model
Customer Account
The entity in the data model that owns wallets and portfolios. Must have one associated identity (KYC/KYB). Linked to one or more user logins via RBAC. Replaces the earlier "workspace" terminology. The customer account is the owner of assets; the user login is the person interacting with the product.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Default Wallet
A special wallet (legacy concept) on the Ledger table used by staking and some exchange processes. Currently the only wallet supported for staking; its role is under review in the context of the portfolio model.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Identity (KYC / KYB)
Regulatory identity data for a person or legal entity. Decoupled from both user login and customer account in the target model. KYB is attached to the overarching business account. Identities can also represent related parties, beneficiaries, advisors, and other non-customer entities where required.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Portfolio
A grouping of wallets — not a customer account or access-control boundary. Used to organise and manage wallets differently (e.g. margin vs non-margin). An entire portfolio can be used as collateral for margin. Distinct from a sub-account: portfolios group wallets for operational purposes; sub-accounts provide legal and governance segregation.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
Wallet
The asset-holding primitive owned by a customer account and grouped into portfolios. In the new model, clearly owned by the customer account (not tied to a user login). Historically overloaded with the term "account" — prefer "wallet" when referring to the asset-holding entity.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
5 · Workflows & Operations
Approval Gate
New
A point in a workflow where an action requires explicit authorisation before it can proceed. Approval gates are configurable by threshold (e.g. trades above $1M), action type (e.g. all withdrawals), or firm policy. See also: Approval Threshold, Multi-Approver / Quorum.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Approval Queue
New
The list of pending items awaiting authorisation, managed by the Controller / Approver sub-type of the Governance + Compliance role. Items have configurable time limits; if not actioned within the limit, they auto-reject or escalate based on business-level policy.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Approval Threshold
New
A configurable limit — typically a transaction or trade size — above which additional approval is required before the action proceeds. Can be set at user level (individual limits) or business level (global minimums). Both levels may apply simultaneously.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Beneficial Owner
New
The underlying person or entity that owns the assets, distinct from the intermediary or operator who manages them on their behalf. The beneficial owner may have no platform access at all — the report or statement may be their only window into their holdings. See also: Indirect (Downstream) Customer.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Bulk Collection
New
The programmatic sweep of funds from multiple sub-accounts into a master account. Used by Programmatic Operators for settlement or redemption events. May be scheduled (e.g. end-of-day) or triggered by a specific redemption event. Partial failures are flagged for exception handling.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Bulk Distribution
New
The programmatic allocation of funds from a master account to multiple sub-accounts simultaneously. Triggered by a deposit event (single or consolidated) or a scheduled sweep. Can be rules-based and automatic, or operator-initiated. Partial failures are flagged for exception handling.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Exception Handling
New
The workflow for investigating and resolving failed automated operations — e.g. failed API calls, rejected transfers, balance mismatches, or KYC flags. Resolution path depends on operator permission level; compliance flags (sanctions, PEP) always escalate to senior compliance regardless of level.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Fund Mandate
New
The documented investment guidelines, asset constraints, risk limits, and objectives for a specific fund or client account. Governs what trades and movements are permissible within that account. Mandate breaches are surfaced as alerts during performance monitoring.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Multi-Approver / Quorum
New
An approval pattern requiring sign-off from a defined number of approvers (e.g. 2-of-3) before an action proceeds. Commonly applied to large withdrawals or fund movements. Configurable per firm. The action remains in the approval queue until the quorum is reached or the time limit expires.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Rebalancing
New
The process of adjusting a portfolio's or fund's asset allocation back to target weights, triggered by drift detection or a scheduled review. Typically requires manager approval followed by trader execution. Individual trades within the rebalance may be subject to approval thresholds.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
Redemption
New
A client's full or partial withdrawal of their investment from a fund or sub-account. May require positions to be liquidated first if insufficient cash balance is held. Subject to approval policy and may be scheduled (periodic redemption) or on-demand.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
6 · Compliance & Regulation
AML (Anti-Money Laundering)
The set of regulatory controls and procedures for detecting and preventing money laundering. On the platform, AML obligations drive transaction monitoring, suspicious activity reporting (SAR), and the compliance officer workflows in the Governance + Compliance role.
Source: Luno Institutional IA; regulatory requirement
KYB (Know Your Business)
Verification process for legal entities (companies, funds, trusts). Applied at the Business Account level. Includes verification of company registration, beneficial ownership, directors, UBOs, and the nature of the business. Distinct from KYC which applies to individuals.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
KYC (Know Your Customer)
Verification process for individuals. Required for users who trade or move funds; may not be required for strictly view-only roles. For Programmatic Operators, each sub-account may require its own KYC at scale, driving distinct onboarding and compliance monitoring workflows.
Source: Sub-accounts Persona and resources; Luno Institutional IA
PEP (Politically Exposed Person)
An individual who holds or has held a prominent public position, or is closely associated with one. PEP status requires enhanced due diligence. A PEP match in screening always triggers immediate account freeze and escalation to senior compliance — it cannot be self-cleared by an operator regardless of permission level.
Source: Luno Institutional IA; regulatory requirement
Sanctions Screening
New
Automated checks run against international sanctions lists (e.g. OFAC, UN, EU) to identify prohibited individuals, entities, or jurisdictions. Any match results in immediate account freeze and mandatory escalation to senior compliance. Cannot be overridden at the operator level. See also: PEP.
Source: Luno Institutional IA; regulatory requirement
Segregation of Duties
New
The compliance principle that a person who can trade or move funds on an account must not also hold approval or oversight authority over the same accounts. Enforced at the system level (not just by policy) in the Governance + Compliance role. A trader cannot approve their own withdrawal; an admin cannot trade.
Source: Luno Institutional IA
7 · Deprecated Terms
Workspace
Deprecated
Earlier concept for a space housing resources like wallets. Explicitly dropped in favour of the term "Customer Account" in the new glossary and architecture. Do not use in new product copy, API documentation, or design artefacts.
Source: B2B 2026 Workshop Day 2
